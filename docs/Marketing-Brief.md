# ElateTrips — Marketing Brief

*Internal reference for the marketing team: positioning, audiences, messaging,
feature benefits, and a ready-to-use copy bank.*

---

## 1. Positioning

**Category:** Celebration-first travel planning (India).

**One-liner:** *India's celebration-first travel planner — book the stay and the
celebration in one go.*

**The problem we solve:** Planning a celebration trip means juggling a hotel
site, a cab app, a florist, a baker and an event planner — and hoping it all
lands on the day. ElateTrips brings the **stay, the setup, the gifts, the
experiences and the travel** into a **single booking flow**.

**Why it's different:** Other travel products sell a *room*. ElateTrips sells the
*moment* — the decor, the surprise, the dinner, the photoshoot — booked together.

## 3. Key messages (lead with these)

1. **One booking for the whole celebration** — not just a room.
2. **Surprise-ready** — setups prepared *before* the guest of honour arrives.
3. **Everything in one cart** — stay, celebration setup, gifts and travel health kits.
4. **Tailored to the occasion** — up to four occasions per trip, each its own day.
5. **Beautiful & effortless** — themeable experience, password-light sign-in.

## 5. Differentiators (the "only ElateTrips" list)

- **Celebration-first, not room-first** — the occasion drives the entire plan.
- **Surprise execution** — a real operational capability with partner properties,
  not just a checkbox.
- **Bundled commerce** — gifts and health kits ride along in the same cart.

## 7. Partner story (B2B angle)

ElateTrips is also a **demand channel for hotels and celebration vendors**.
A property submits a short, no-commitment **Expression of Interest** covering the
services it can offer (decor packages, cabs, on-ground services like
photographers/makeup/planners/guides, gifts, medical kits), its **surprise-setup
capability**, and how it keeps **rates & availability current**. Submissions reach
the partnerships team with a reference number.

**B2B hooks:** "Turn celebrations into bookings." · "List your property where
people plan the moment, not just the room."

## 9. Launch notes

- **Geography:** lead all launch messaging with **Ooty**; tease Coorg/Munnar/Goa
  as "coming soon" to build a waitlist.
- **Seasonality:** lean into peak gifting/celebration moments (birthdays,
  anniversaries, wedding season, festivals).
- **Proof:** favour real setups and surprise reveals in creative — the surprise
  capability is the most differentiated, share-worthy story.
- **CTA:** "Start planning" for users; "Partner with us" for hotels.
